A-Level Economics Diagrams

Diagrams are worth significant marks in A-Level Economics. EconFlow helps you study the most important graphs, from supply and demand to complex market failure and AD/AS diagrams.

Why Diagrams Matter

In your economics exams, a well-drawn, clearly labelled diagram can often replace a paragraph of text and secure higher level analysis marks. Knowing how to draw and interpret them is essential.

Essential Microeconomics Diagrams

  • Supply and Demand: The foundation. Shifts vs movements along the curve.
  • Market Failure: Externalities (positive and negative), public goods, and the tragedy of the commons.
  • Market Structures: Perfect competition, monopolistic competition, oligopoly (kinked demand curve), and monopoly.
  • Government Intervention: Tariffs, subsidies, maximum/minimum prices, and taxation.

Essential Macroeconomics Diagrams

  • AD/AS Diagrams: Keynesian vs Classical long-run aggregate supply.
  • Phillips Curve: Short-run vs long-run trade-offs between inflation and unemployment.
  • Lorenz Curve: Measuring inequality and the Gini coefficient.
  • Exchange Rates: Floating vs fixed exchange rate mechanisms.
